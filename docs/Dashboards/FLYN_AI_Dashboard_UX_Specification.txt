FLYN AI — Dashboard UX Specification
This document defines the authoritative UX structure, hierarchy, and behavioral rules for all
dashboards within the FLYN AI platform. It serves as the single source of truth for product,
design, and engineering teams.

1. Design Philosophy
All dashboards in FLYN AI follow a unified, plugin-compatible structure. Modules differ only by
data, KPIs, and actions — never by layout or interaction principles. This ensures scalability,
consistency, and minimal cognitive load for users.

2. Universal Dashboard Structure
2.1 Header KPI Strip
Purpose: Provide instant operational awareness.
Rules: 3–6 KPIs maximum, clickable, real or demo data depending on plan.
Examples: Pipeline Value, Active Calls, Attendance Rate.

2.2 Primary Action Bar
Purpose: Highlight the most important actions available.
Rules: One primary action, up to two secondary actions, AI actions grouped visually.

2.3 Core Operational Table
Purpose: Primary working area of the dashboard.
Rules: Sortable, filterable, bulk actions enabled, saved views supported.

2.4 Contextual Analytics
Purpose: Explain performance trends.
Rules: 3–5 charts maximum, context-sensitive, mandatory time-range selector.

2.5 AI Insight Panel
Purpose: Provide intelligent, actionable guidance.
Rules: Collapsible, non-intrusive, clearly labeled as AI-generated.

2.6 Activity & System Feed
Purpose: Transparency and auditability.
Rules: Chronological, human-readable, linked to system entities.

3. Dashboard Types & Content Mapping
Each module defines its KPIs, primary tables, actions, and AI insights while inheriting the
universal structure.
- CRM: Deals and Contacts tables, pipeline KPIs, deal intelligence.
- Unified Inbox: Conversations table, SLA metrics, AI reply assistance.
- Telephony: Call logs, call analytics, AI summaries.
- HR: Employee directory, attendance KPIs, attrition insights.
- Church/Community: Member management, attendance tracking, engagement trends.
- Freelance/Agency: Client and project tracking, revenue KPIs, workload balance.

4. Role-Based Visibility Rules
Founder/Admin: Full system visibility including financials, analytics, and governance.
Manager/Ops: Team-level KPIs and operational analytics.
Agent/Staff: Task-level views and assigned records only.

5. Plugin Compatibility Rule
New modules must define only their data schemas, KPIs, actions, and AI insights. All layout,
permissions, analytics containers, and interactions are inherited automatically.

6. Brand Kit Integration
The FLYN AI brand kit must include dashboard grid layouts, KPI strip styles, table standards,
chart usage guidelines, AI panel behavior, and empty or demo state definitions.

7. Governing Principle
Consistency is a feature. Dashboards must feel instantly familiar across all modules while
remaining powerful enough to scale to enterprise complexity.
